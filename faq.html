Puppy Care Basics

How often should I take my puppy to the vet?

Puppies should visit the vet every 3-4 weeks until they're 16 weeks old for vaccinations and health checks. After that, annual wellness exams are recommended.

What vaccinations does my puppy need?

Core vaccines include distemper, parvovirus, hepatitis, and rabies. Your vet will create a vaccination schedule based on your puppy's age and health status.

When should I start training my puppy?

Begin basic training as soon as you bring your puppy home, typically around 8 weeks old. Focus on socialization, potty training, and simple commands.

How much exercise does a puppy need?

Puppies need 5 minutes of exercise per month of age, twice daily. For example, a 3-month-old puppy needs about 15 minutes of exercise, two times a day.

Which breed is best for first-time owners?

Popular choices for beginners include Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, Cavalier King Charles Spaniels, and Beagles due to their friendly nature and trainability.

What breeds are good for families with children?

Family-friendly breeds include Golden Retrievers, Labrador Retrievers, Beagles, Bulldogs, and Poodles. Always supervise interactions between children and puppies.
